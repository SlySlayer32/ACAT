Statement in Support of Application to Vary Termination Date

Applicants: Jacob and Erinn
Requested order: vary the termination date from 31 March 2026 to 28 April 2026

[Household Circumstances]
1. We are a household of two adults, Jacob and Erinn, and three children aged 9 years, 6 years and 4 months.
2. We have 13 years of continuous rental history, consisting of 3 years at the current property and 10 years at the previous property.
3. Throughout that period, we have aimed to be stable and reliable tenants.

[Orders Sought]
4. We ask the Tribunal to vary the current termination date of 31 March 2026 and extend the tenancy to 28 April 2026.
5. The extension sought is short, specific, and intended to allow an orderly move while reducing hardship to our children and avoiding unnecessary prejudice to the landlord.

[Financial Capacity]
6. Our current household income from Centrelink is $5,056 per month, paid fortnightly, and we rely on that income as guaranteed income for the family (see Exhibit C).
7. Based on our written household budget, we can comfortably afford rent of approximately $600 to $650 per week while still saving approximately $300 to $500 per month (see Exhibit C).
8. We have also recently signed a cleaning contract, and any income from that work is in addition to the guaranteed income already disclosed in our income material (see Exhibit C).
9. We structure our rent to be paid before we receive our income so that rent is prioritised and paid first.
10. Our payment history, ledger material and backpay records show our efforts to comply with our tenancy obligations and payment arrangements (see Exhibit B).

[Housing Search]
11. We have been actively trying to secure alternative accommodation and have applied for multiple properties without success (see Exhibit A).
12. Those unsuccessful applications and related records show that we are not delaying the move by choice, but are continuing to search for housing in a difficult rental market (see Exhibit A).

[Medical Hardship]
13. A central reason for this application is the medical needs of our eldest daughter.
14. Our 9-year-old daughter has a long-awaited autism assessment booked for 31 March 2026, being the same day as the current termination date (see Exhibit D).
15. We have been trying for several years to obtain this assessment, and it is a significant and time-critical appointment for her care and support.
16. The assessment costs more than $3,000, and we understand that if it is cancelled because of the move it may take a further six months or more to secure another appointment (see Exhibit E).
17. We are required to spend 30 March 2026 and 31 March 2026 attending appointments and travelling for that assessment, making it practically impossible to complete packing, secure a new property and move out on the same date (see Exhibits D and E).
18. The overlap between the assessment and the termination date has caused significant stress and instability for our children because their home environment and sense of safety are being disrupted at the same time as this important medical event.

[Practical Moving Issues]
19. Because of this timing clash, we have fallen behind on packing and organising the move.
20. We need additional time to arrange removalists, storage and professional cleaning so the property can be returned in good condition (see Exhibit H).
21. A four-week extension would let us secure suitable alternative accommodation instead of accepting any property out of desperation.
22. It would also allow us to properly pack and consolidate our belongings, coordinate removalists safely around the assessment, and return the property clean and ready without avoidable delay (see Exhibit H).

[Prior Hearing and Previous Materials]
23. At the earlier ACAT hearing, we did not have a proper opportunity to present our full budget and explain that we can afford market rent at approximately $600 per week while still maintaining savings (see Exhibit C).
24. If that financial position had been fully explained with the supporting documents now attached, we believe a more realistic termination date than 31 March 2026 may have been set.

[Landlord Impact]
25. To minimise any disadvantage to the landlord, we are willing for the property to be advertised immediately and for Belle Property to conduct inspections and viewings while we remain in residence during the extension period (see Exhibit G).
26. That would allow replacement tenants to be identified and approved before the proposed move-out date of 28 April 2026, reducing any vacancy risk.

[Conclusion]
27. For those reasons, namely our rental history, active housing search, demonstrated financial capacity, our daughter's time-critical autism assessment, the practical need to arrange removalists and cleaning, and our willingness to cooperate with advertising and inspections, we respectfully ask the Tribunal to vary the termination date to 28 April 2026.
